# Reflection

The biggest thing this project drilled in: GIGO is real. When the persona description is vague, the model sounds like generic AI. When you give it real research, real examples, and real constraints, it actually sounds like a person.

The most interesting lesson was about source selection. For Abhimanyu, LinkedIn alone would've given a polished but completely wrong voice. Twitter showed the real register - punchy, builder-obsessed, LFG energy. Using the wrong source means correct structure, wrong input, wrong output. That's GIGO at the research stage, not the coding stage.

Few-shot examples were the single most effective change across all three personas. They don't just show what to say - they show sentence rhythm, what to end with, how to build a point. Structure alone without examples is scaffolding with no building.

What I'd do differently: more voice samples per persona, and a small eval harness - fixed test questions, tone checklist - so future tuning is less "does this feel right?" and more systematic.

The model is capable of real fidelity. The bottleneck is always the quality of what you put in.
